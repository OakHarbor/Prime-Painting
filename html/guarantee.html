Our Guarantee

5 Year Warranty

What does our 5 year workmanship guarnatee cover?

Our guarantee covers products and materials used and the quality of work done while the paint manufacturer covers their products. So if the paint is the reaoson for a part of the job failing, that will be covered by the paint manufacturer's warranty. But if the are issues with the paint and it is because of the workmanship of the painters, that is covered by us.

How long is the average warranty guarantee?

There are many contractors who offer a wide variety of warranties from 1-2 years to 3-4 years and inbetween. However, according to Nevada State Law, a contractor must provide a 4 year minimum guarantee ont their work. Any contractors who advertise less are most likely unaware of state law requiring them to have a 4 year minimum.

Prime Painting goes the extra mile - we're proud to offer a 5 year guarantee for all our work. In our work, we always believe in going the extra mile, so we also do that with our guarnate and going above and beyond the minimum.

How you are protected from accidents or damage from a contractor?

Every contractor in the state of Nevada that says he's a contractor must have a license authorized by the state. If they perform poor work and have enough complaints to the licensing board they can have that license takne away and can no longer operate as a contractor. So when you are delaing with someone who claims to be a contractor, make sure you get their license number and look it up on the state's website to make sure they are in good standing.

In addition to being required ot have a state issued contractor's license, we are also required ot have insurance which protects homeowners from expenses incurred when something on the job goes horribly wrong. So on top of making sure a contrcator is licensed, you should also make sure they are insured.

As if that wasn't enough, we are also required to have workman's compensation insurance protect out workers when on the job. If they get hurt in your home, the liability doesn't fall on you. Both of these insurances are vital to any contracting project as it shifts liability from you and your homeowners insurance to the contractor that is performing the work. And if a contractor does not adher to the state required 4 year workmanship warranty then you can go straight to the licensing board and file a complaint if it is within the 4 years.
