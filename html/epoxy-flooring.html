Expoxy Floor Installation

Installing a New Epoxy Floor

The garage floor is often not thought about when people are planning to renovate their homes, but resurfacing your garage floors with a new epoxy floor is a great investemnt in your home by making it safer, more functional, and increase the resale value of your home. Prime Painting does more that just painting, we also specialize in installing new epoxy flooring for Las Vegas and the surrounding area.

Benefits Of Installing Epoxy Flooring

  • Epoxy coatings create a smooth surface with no cracks gaps or seams and can withstand grease, scuff reisistant, impervious to moisture and chemical spills, and are highly unlikely to chip or peel like many other garage floor paints.

  • The shiny gloss surface reflects more light - making the area brighter and more vibrant.

  • Hard a durable that can withstand a large amount of traffic and impacts.

  • Relatively easy and quick to install since we don't have to design a layout, cut our adhesives, or use specilized equipment.

  • Can be applied to have differnt patterns and styles to match any persons design pallate.

  • Protects the existing concrete flooring underneath it from wear and tear and cracking

Epoxy Flooring Preparation

If you want to install a new epoxy floor coating, the existing surface needs to be clean and a little pourous so we can make sure the coating can adhere to the concrete properly because epozy flooring does not adhere well to polished or sealed concrete. It's also very important to remove all grease and repair any cracks or chips in the concrete before you begin.

Which Epoxy To Use

  • Solid Epoxy: This is the purest form of epoxy to use as it contains no solvents that can evaporate. However, it's very expensive. And as a result of it's ability to harden rather quickly, it is also VERY difficult to handle and should only be done by an experienced professional.

  • Water-base Epoxy: Water-base epoxy contains about 40-60% solid material and this type of epoxy doesn't carry hazardous solvent fumes. They are becoming very popular and are available at most hardware stores.

  • Solvent-base Epoxy: Much like water-base epoxy, solvent-base epoxy contains about 40-60% solids and are available is a variety of colors. However, you need to wear a respirator during the application process because the solvents' fumes are powerful and hazardous if breathed in and requires the garage to be ventilated and keeping pets and children away from it while drying.

  • Color Flakes: You can add a lot more variety to your epoxy floors by using pre-tinted or metallic floor coverings which come in the form of flakes you put in the second layer. This creates a speckled pattern of color and texture to add more color and variety to your garage floors.

Maintaining Your Epoxy Floors

  • Vacuum and sweep up any debris that may be on the floor.

  • If you spill something on it you should immeidately wipe it up with a soft cloth.

  • If you need to deep clean any stains or spills, mop them up with a mixture of 1/2 cup ammonia to 1 gallon of water.

  • Remove any rust simply by using hot water and a cloth. Do not use an abrasive cleaner, acids, or other unfamiliar chemicals on your epoxy floor.

  • Th easiest way to clean them is to hose them down with water and wipe it away into the driveway with a squeegee on a long pole.

  • For anything else, consult a professional.
